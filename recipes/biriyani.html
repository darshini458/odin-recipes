Biryani Recipe

Ingredients

For the Rice:

  • Basmati rice: 2 cups
  • Water: 4 cups
  • Bay leaves: 2
  • Cloves: 4
  • Green cardamom: 4
  • Cinnamon stick: 1 inch
  • Salt: 1 teaspoon

For the Meat Marinade:

  • Meat (chicken, mutton, or beef): 500 grams
  • Yogurt: 1 cup
  • Ginger-garlic paste: 2 tablespoons
  • Red chili powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Garam masala: 1 teaspoon
  • Lemon juice: 1 tablespoon
  • Salt: to taste

For the Biryani Masala:

  • Oil or ghee: 4 tablespoons
  • Onions: 2 large, thinly sliced
  • Tomatoes: 2 large, chopped
  • Green chilies: 4, slit
  • Ginger-garlic paste: 2 tablespoons
  • Biryani masala powder: 2 tablespoons
  • Coriander powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Cumin powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Red chili powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Salt: to taste

Additional Ingredients:

  • Fresh coriander leaves: 1/2 cup, chopped
  • Fresh mint leaves: 1/2 cup, chopped
  • Saffron strands: a pinch (optional)
  • Warm milk: 2 tablespoons (for saffron)
  • Fried onions: 1/2 cup (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges: for garnish
  • Hard-boiled eggs: 2 (optional, for garnish)

For Layering:

  • Fried onions: 1/2 cup
  • Fresh coriander leaves: 1/4 cup, chopped
  • Fresh mint leaves: 1/4 cup, chopped
  • Ghee or butter: 2 tablespoons

Optional Ingredients:

  • Raisins: 2 tablespoons (optional)
  • Cashew nuts: 2 tablespoons (optional)

Preparation Steps:

  1. Marinate the meat with the marinade ingredients and let it sit for at least 1 hour.
  2. Cook the basmati rice with water, bay leaves, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and salt until 70% cooked. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large pot, heat oil or ghee and fry the onions until golden brown. Add the ginger-garlic paste, tomatoes, green chilies, and the rest of the spices. Cook until the oil separates.
  4. Add the marinated meat and cook until the meat is tender.
  5. In a separate pot, layer the biryani starting with a layer of rice, followed by the cooked meat mixture, then sprinkle some fried onions, coriander leaves, and mint leaves. Repeat the layers.
  6. Dissolve the saffron in warm milk and drizzle over the top layer.
  7. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat (dum) for about 30-40 minutes.
  8. Garnish with fried onions, fresh coriander leaves, mint leaves, lemon wedges, and boiled eggs before serving.
